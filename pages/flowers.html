Zendu Plant

Origin: Native to the Americas, with a high concentration of species in Southern Mexico. It was introduced to other parts of the world after the Spanish conquest of the Aztec Empire.
Growth: Most cultivated varieties are annual herbs, though some wild species are perennial. They can grow from 0.1 to 2.2 meters tall depending on the species.
Leaves: Pinnately compound, green leaves that are deeply divided into leaflets. When crushed, the leaves have a distinct and pungent aroma. Flowers: Composite flower heads that contain both ray and disc florets. Blooms come in bright shades of golden, orange, yellow, and sometimes maroon.
